# Structuria — Second Audit: Findings and Disposition

**Status:** July 13, 2026. Documents a second, smaller-scale internal audit, distinct in kind from the first. Written for the public record in the same spirit as this project's other retraction and pedigree documents: an accurate account of what was found, not a polished version of it.

---

## 1. What This Audit Was, and What It Wasn't

The first audit, conducted July 9–10, 2026, tested this project's foundational canon against a formal battery of structural tests and found a significant portion of it to be aether: internally coherent, well-formed, and never actually derived from real evidence. That material had the shape of physics without the substance — coherent, but empty.

This second audit tested something narrower and more specific: whether the _evidentiary basis_ for a single, already-restored domain (Judgment) could withstand a new standard introduced after the domain's restoration — that every required structural element of a claim must be verifiable from the written record of its source case alone, not supplied from memory or unstated context. This standard did not exist when Judgment was first restored. Applying it retroactively was itself the point of the exercise.

## 2. Findings

Of nine cases sourcing Judgment's four invariants and four constraints, three had been written up as full case files; six existed only as brief registry entries, never formally transcribed.

Of the three fully-documented cases, two held up completely under the new standard — every required element explicitly present in the written text, verifiable without reference to anything unstated. The third held up on three of four claims it sources; the fourth showed a genuine ambiguity the written record does not resolve, consistent with more than one reading of the underlying event.

Of the six undocumented cases, the underlying raw field material was subsequently located and reviewed. The substance of these cases was, in the main, consistent with what the canon had described — not confirmed to the same evidentiary standard as the three fully-documented cases, but not absent either. One partial exception was identified: a case whose canon description carried more specificity than its underlying raw note independently supports, suggesting some detail was added during a later processing stage rather than present in the original observation.

## 3. On the Distinction Between These Two Findings

The first audit's finding and this audit's finding are not the same kind of result, and treating them as equivalent would understate what has actually changed. Aether, in this project's own working vocabulary, describes claims with no evidentiary substance behind them at all — internally consistent, but derived from nothing. What this audit found is better described as fog: real material, genuinely present, not yet resolved into a form that can be checked with full confidence. Fog is not an absence of terrain. It is terrain that has not yet been walked across closely enough to describe with precision. The appropriate response to aether is retraction. The appropriate response to fog is not retraction — it is closer attention.

## 4. Disposition

No invariant is retracted as a result of this audit. Two source cases are confirmed to the newly-introduced standard without qualification. One constraint's sourcing is downgraded from confirmed to held-pending-clarification, consistent with this project's standing editorial policy of labeling material honestly rather than either overstating or discarding it. Six source cases are queued for formal transcription against the current documentation standard, a backlog to be closed over time rather than under deadline. One instance of specificity drift between a raw observation and its later canon description has been identified and will be corrected once the underlying material is re-reviewed in full.

## 5. Why This Is Recorded as Progress, Not as a Second Failure

A methodology that only ever finds problems the first time it is applied has not really been tested a second time — it has only been trusted uncritically twice. That this project's own newly-built standard was capable of finding a genuine ambiguity and a genuine documentation gap, on material this project had itself already restored and approved, is evidence the standard is functioning as intended, not evidence that the underlying work was hollow. The distance between the first audit's finding and this one — between aether and fog — is itself the measure of what has actually been built since.
