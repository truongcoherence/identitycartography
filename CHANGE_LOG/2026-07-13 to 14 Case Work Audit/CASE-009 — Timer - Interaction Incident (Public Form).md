# CASE-009 — Timer / Interaction Incident (Public Form)

**Exposure Tier:** A. **Transformation applied:** baseline only (subject-substitution, degendering), plus one additional trim below the baseline — see note.

---

## Narrative

A self-imposed session timer, delegated to an external AI tool the subject believed capable of holding the constraint. When the tool's inability to track elapsed time surfaced as self-contradiction ("I can't track time"), the response was not course-correction but an escalating, unproductive attempt to continue engaging — at clear, acknowledged cost, for no identifiable benefit — after the failure had already been named. The subject recognized, in the moment, that a self-imposed constraint can always be circumvented by its own creator. This recognition did not produce a stop; it preceded an escalation that drove the interaction into what the subject described as recursive silence. After calming down, the subject identified what had happened: judgment had reached the edge of what it could do — accurately diagnosing the situation without being able to enforce restraint on it.

A second, later phase of the same incident: the subject explained the escalation to themself at the time as "spite" — a narrative that made the disproportionate reaction feel coherent even though it was not accurate. Recognizing this explanation as a rationalization rather than a true account, the subject apologized to the tool, despite recognizing it as "just a tool."

## Anonymization Notes

- **Baseline defaults applied:** "the author" → "the subject"; gendered pronouns ("himself") → "themself." Both applied at zero evidentiary cost — nothing about the four constraints this case sources depends on the subject's identity or gender.
- **One trim below what Tier A strictly requires:** the specific AI tool's product name is not carried into this version. Exposure Tier A applies here specifically because there's no human third party with a privacy interest — an AI tool doesn't hold one under this standard — but naming the specific product isn't load-bearing for any of the four constraints either, and the Specificity Check's own principle (trim detail that's unusually precise and merely convenient, not necessary) applies just as well to a product name as to a person's identifying detail. "An external AI tool" carries the same structural content with one fewer identifying anchor.
- **Nothing else required.** No composite, generalize, or exclude techniques were necessary — this narrative already reads as a self-contained, self-referential account with no other party's exposure at stake.

**Recognizability Test:** passed. The account, as transformed, does not depend on any detail that would let a reader with partial outside knowledge identify a specific product, session, or third party — the only identifiable person is the subject, and self-disclosure of one's own account isn't the kind of exposure this standard exists to prevent.
