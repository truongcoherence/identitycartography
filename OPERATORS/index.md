# OPERATORS
Operators are the structural tools and lenses that help you understand situations inside Identity Cartography. 

- They do *not* describe identity physics. 
- They do *not* model behavior. 
- They do *not* govern cognition.

Operators show you:
* how to see structure clearly
* how situations behave
* how identity behaves
* which tool to use when you need clarity

Identity Cartography uses three types of operators, each serving a different purpose:

———-

## 1. Core Operators 

These are the five tools you actually use. They help you see structure, interpret signals, reduce load, detect instability, and test boundaries.
You will find them here:

* [LENS](../OPERATORS/Lens%20Prompt.md)
* [INTERPRETER](../OPERATORS/Interpreter%20Prompt.md)
* [RELIEF](../OPERATORS/Relief%20Prompt.md)
* [SEISMOGRAPH](../OPERATORS/ Seismograph%20Prompt.md)
* [PROBE](../OPERATORS/PROBE%20Prompt.md)

Each Core Operator has:
* a clear definition
* a usage guide
* an IFU (Instruction for Use)
* prompts you can run
* examples of correct usage

These are the only operators that are *procedural*.

———-
## 2. Compass 
Compass is the meta‑operator. 

Compass helps you:
* anchor context
* stabilize perspective
* maintain orientation across complexity

***Compass is an operator that helps you determine which core operator to use.***

You will find it here:
* [COMPASS](../OPERATORS/Compass%20Prompt.md)

————

## 3. Situation Operators
Situation Operators describe how situations behave under environmental physics.

- They are *not* tools. 
- They are *not* prompts. 
- They are *not* actionable.

They help you understand:
* how time behaves (Clock)
* how position behaves (Locator)
* how form behaves (Form)

These will live here:
- [Clock](../OPERATORS/Situation/Clock.md)
- [Locator](../OPERATORS/Situation/Locator.md)
- [Form](OPERATORS/Situation/Form.md)

***These operators give you structural context, not instructions.***

——---

## 4. Identity Operators

Identity Operators describe how identity behaves under different conditions.
- They are *not* tools. 
- They are *not* prompts. 
- They are *not* actionable.

They help you understand:
* identity under uncertainty (Arbiter)
* identity under protection (Gate)
* identity under shared load (Binder)
* identity across time (Carrier)
* identity under consequence (Exposed)

These will live here:

- [Arbiter](../OPERATORS/Identity/Arbiter.md)
- [Gate](../OPERATORS/Identity/Gate.md)
- [Binder](../OPERATORS/Identity/Binder.md)
- [Carrier](../OPERATORS/Identity/Carrier.md)
- [Exposed](../OPERATORS/Identity/Exposed.md)

***These operators help you recognize identity behavior, not regulate it.***
