# Preamble

#### Scope:  
Quantum governs micro‑scale environmental possibility. It defines the smallest units of environmental state, the discrete transitions available within those states, and the local conditions under which potential becomes actual. It does not govern identity, intention, interpretation, or decision.

#### Intent:  
To articulate the irreducible micro‑structures that determine what can form, what can change, and what cannot stabilize. Quantum establishes the possibility space within the macro constraints set by Geophysics.

#### Guardrails:  
This canon must not be read as psychological, moral, or prescriptive. It does not describe preference, agency, or cognition. It prohibits interpreting micro‑states as metaphor, narrative, or personality. It describes environmental possibility, not identity behavior.

#  I. CANON LAYER

---

## 1. Definition & Domain

Quantum is the architectural layer that defines micro‑scale possibility.  
It establishes:

- the smallest units of environmental state
    
- the discrete transitions available within those states
    
- the local constraints that govern change
    
- the micro‑conditions under which structure can form
    

Quantum operates at the scale of possibility, not outcome.  
It determines what can occur before identity interacts with the environment.

---

## 2. Environmental Forces

Quantum forces are discrete, local, and invariant.

- State Discreteness — All micro‑conditions exist in discrete states.
    
- Local Constraint — Micro‑states are bounded by immediate conditions.
    
- Transition Cost — Movement between states requires energy.
    
- Indeterminacy — Multiple states remain possible until constrained.
    
- Superposition — Micro‑states can coexist until resolved.
    
- Collapse — Resolution occurs when environmental conditions force a single state.
    
- Locality — Effects propagate only through adjacent micro‑states.
    

These forces describe micro‑environmental behavior, not identity behavior.

---

## 3. Boundary Conditions

Quantum stability arises when:

- micro‑states remain within local tolerance
    
- transitions occur within available energy
    
- superpositions resolve predictably
    
- locality is preserved
    

Quantum instability arises when:

- micro‑states exceed tolerance
    
- transitions require unavailable energy
    
- superpositions persist beyond structural limits
    
- locality breaks
    

Boundary conditions determine whether possibility stabilizes or collapses.

---

## 4. Phenomena (Emergent Patterns)

Quantum phenomena arise when superposition + decoherence + collapse interact under micro‑scale constraint.

### Identity Superposition

Coexistence of incompatible micro‑states prior to environmental resolution.

### Collapse Under Observation

Forced stabilization of a micro‑state when environmental conditions require a single outcome.

### Decoherence Drift

Gradual loss of micro‑state stability as unresolved states disperse under local constraint.

### State Tunneling

Transition between non‑adjacent micro‑states without passing through intermediate states when local barriers are bypassed.

### Micro‑Fracture

Small coherence breaks within micro‑structure that accumulate into larger instability.

### Observer‑Induced Distortion

Alteration of micro‑state configuration when environmental attention modifies local conditions.

### Quantum Lock‑In

Stabilization of a fragile micro‑state through repeated environmental reinforcement.

### Micro‑Friction Amplification

Escalation of small local resistances into structural instability under repeated micro‑pressure.

Phenomena Breadth:  
Quantum phenomena are narrow in scope but enormous in consequence, determining the stability of all higher‑order structures.

Identity’s viable actions are shaped by micro‑conditions, not intention.  
Identity’s futures are constrained by what can form at the smallest scale.

This section describes identity under micro‑physics, not identity behavior.

# II. PHYSICS LAYER

---

## 1. Primitives

### a. Discrete State

All micro‑conditions exist in discrete units.  
Remove discreteness → no micro‑structure → Quantum collapses.

### b. Locality

Micro‑states interact only with adjacent states.  
Remove locality → no constraint → environment becomes non‑physical.

### c. Indeterminacy

Multiple states remain possible until constrained.  
Remove indeterminacy → no superposition → no collapse dynamics.

These primitives define the activation conditions of the domain.

---

## 2. Invariants

### a. Possibility Is Bounded

Micro‑states exist within fixed limits.  
Remove bounds → no structure.

### b. Transition Requires Energy

State change incurs cost.  
Remove cost → no stability.

### c. Collapse Is Forced

Unresolved states must resolve under constraint.  
Remove collapse → no resolution fields.

### d. Local Conditions Dominate

Micro‑states are governed by immediate environment.  
Remove locality → no micro‑physics.

These invariants define the physics of micro‑scale possibility.

---

## 3. Structural Distortions

### a. Continuity as Possibility (Form Distortion)

Mistaking smooth experience for continuous micro‑structure.  
Occurs because discreteness is below perceptual resolution.

### b. Abundance as Freedom (Function Distortion)

Mistaking many micro‑states for unconstrained possibility.  
Occurs because transition cost is invisible until activated.

These distortions arise from misreading micro‑scale geometry.
