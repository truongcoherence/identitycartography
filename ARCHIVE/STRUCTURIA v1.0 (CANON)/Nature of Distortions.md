**Structural Distortions — Overview Artifact (Canon)**

**Purpose**

Structural Distortions identify the **predictable mis‑sees** produced by the geometry of each Identity Domain.  
They are not psychological errors, personal misunderstandings, or cultural habits.  
They are **physics‑level artifacts**: the ways a domain’s _form_ and _function_ bend perception.

Structural Distortions define the **negative space** of a domain.  
They show what the domain is routinely mistaken for because of how it is shaped.

---

**Definition**

**A Structural Distortion is a systematic mis‑seeing that arises directly from a domain’s form or function.**

It is not interpretive.  
It is not optional.  
It is not observer‑dependent.

It is the predictable perceptual warp created by the domain’s own geometry.

---

**Structure**

Every receptive domain (Boundaries, Cohesion, Stewardship, Peril) produces **two** Structural Distortions:

1. **Form Distortion** — a mis‑seeing of _what the domain is_.
2. **Function Distortion** — a mis‑seeing of _what the domain does_.

Judgment is the sole exception.  
Because it has internal/external form and internal/external function, it produces **four** Structural Distortions — one per surface of the domain.

---

**Why Distortions Matter**

Structural Distortions are the **primary mis‑seeing** a reader must understand.

They:

- define the boundaries of each domain
- prevent category collapse
- protect the physics from moralization or psychologizing
- reveal the exact ways a domain can be confused for something it is not
- provide the canonical “do not mis‑see this as X” guidance

Interpretive misclassifications may vary.  
Structural Distortions do not.

If a reader remembers only one thing about mis‑seeing, it should be the Distortions.
