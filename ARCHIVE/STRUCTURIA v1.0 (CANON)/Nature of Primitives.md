**Primitives vs. Constraints**

**_What They Are, How They Differ, and Why the Distinction Matters_**

---

**⭐** **1. Purpose of This Artifact**

This document establishes the formal distinction between **Primitives** and **Constraints** within Identity Domain Physics.  
The two terms were historically conflated, especially during the early Judgment work, and this artifact resolves that ambiguity permanently.

---

**⭐** **2. Definitions**

**A. Primitives — Irreducible Internal Operations**

**A Primitive is an internal operation the domain must perform in order to exist.**

Primitives describe the **mechanics** of a domain:

- what the domain _does_
- how it _operates_
- what identity _must_ do inside that domain
- the irreducible actions that cannot be decomposed further

Primitives are **structural**, **mechanical**, and **domain‑internal**.

They arise directly from the domain’s **Form** and **Function** invariants.

**Examples**

- Judgment → Discernment, Commitment, Consequence‑binding
- Boundaries → Differentiation, Regulation
- Cohesion → Integration, Load‑distribution
- Stewardship → Continuation, Preservation
- Peril → Exposure

These are not interpretations.  
They are the **physics** of the domain.

---

**B. Constraints — Interpretive Guardrails**

**A Constraint is a clarifying truth that prevents misinterpretation of a domain.**

Constraints describe the **interpretive boundaries** of a domain:

- what the domain _is not_
- what people commonly misread
- what distortions must be prevented
- what assumptions must be corrected

Constraints are **interpretive**, **pedagogical**, and **reader‑facing**.

They arise from the domain’s **Misclassifications** and **Failure Modes**, not its mechanics.

**Examples**

- “Boundaries are not rejection.”
- “Cohesion is not harmony.”
- “Stewardship is not control.”
- “Judgment is not blame.”
- “Peril is not danger.”

These are not mechanics.  
They are **interpretive corrections**.

---

**⭐** **3. Why the Early System Confused the Two**

**Judgment was the first domain with real physics.**

It had:

- internal form
- external form
- internal function
- external function

This produced a **four‑surface geometry**, which collapsed into **three primitives**.

But at the time, you didn’t have the concept of “primitives,” so you called them **constraints**.

**Meanwhile, the other domains were not yet domains.**

They were:

- continents
- terrains
- relational contexts

They had no invariants, no mechanics, and therefore **no primitives**.

So Gemini correctly said:

“The other domains have no primitives.”

Because at that stage, they didn’t.

---

**⭐** **4. Why Gemini Later Copied Judgment’s Primitives Into Other Domains**

Gemini assumed:

“If Judgment has constraints, all domains must have constraints of the same type.”

So it produced Judgment‑shaped constraints for Boundaries:

- “Boundaries fluctuate over space”
- “Boundaries are not rejection”
- “Boundaries are leaky”

These were **interpretive constraints**, not **primitives**.

Gemini was treating primitives as **universal identity constraints**, not **domain‑internal mechanics**.

This artifact corrects that permanently.

---

**⭐** **5. Canonical Distinction (Final Form)**

|**Concept**|**Primitives**|**Constraints**|
|---|---|---|
|**Nature**|Internal mechanics|Interpretive guardrails|
|**Source**|Form + Function invariants|Misclassifications + Failure Modes|
|**Purpose**|Define what the domain _does_|Prevent misreading of what the domain _is_|
|**Scope**|Domain‑specific|Reader‑facing|
|**Status**|Structural|Pedagogical|
|**Example**|“Judgment collapses optionality.”|“Judgment is not blame.”|

This table is stable and canonical.

---

**⭐** **6. Canonical Rule**

**Primitives describe the internal physics of a domain.  
Constraints describe the interpretive boundaries of a domain.  
They are not interchangeable.**

Primitives are **mechanical**.  
Constraints are **interpretive**.

Primitives belong in the **canon**.  
Constraints belong in the **teaching layer**.

---

**⭐** **7. Why This Distinction Matters**

Without this separation:

- domains drift
- primitives become moralized
- constraints become mistaken for physics
- interpretive corrections get mistaken for mechanics
- the system loses derivability
- expansions break symmetry

With this separation:

- the physics stays clean
- the teaching stays clear
- the domains remain derivable
- the system remains extensible
- the canon remains defensible

This is the structural foundation the system needed.
