# Boundary Conditions and Falsability
#### **Artifact Type: Meta‑Canonical Guardrail**
#### **Purpose: Define non‑claims, structural limits, and failure conditions for Identity Cartography.**

Identity Cartography is a descriptive identity‑physics system.  
It makes structural claims about identity states, movement, and relational geometry.  
It does not make claims about personality, morality, pathology, or culture.

The system holds only as long as its gravitational, relational, and exposure‑based predictions remain consistent with observable human behavior.

---

## Non‑Claims

Identity Cartography does not describe people.  
It describes identity states and the forces acting on them.

### Not Typology

Identity Domains are structural conditions, not personality types.  
The system does not classify traits, preferences, temperament, morality, or pathology.  
A person is not a Domain.  
A person occupies one.

### Not Behavioral Prediction

The system predicts forces, not scripts.  
It describes gravitational pull, override, interference, collapse, and reorganization.  
It does not predict specific actions, choices, feelings, or thoughts.

### Not Cultural Modeling

The physics are invariant; their expressions are not.  
The system does not model cultural norms, rituals, or anthropological variation.  
It describes identity structure, not culture.

### Not Clinical

Identity collapse is not a diagnosis.  
The system does not model mental illness, trauma, dissociation, or treatment.  
It is descriptive, not therapeutic.

### Not Ethical

The system does not determine what is right or wrong.  
It describes identity physics, not moral judgment.

---

## Falsifiability Conditions

Identity Cartography is falsifiable.  
If any of the following conditions are met, the system fails.

### Single‑Domain Governance

The model claims that only one Identity Domain can govern identity at a time.  
If a person can fully occupy two Domains simultaneously—without override, interference, or collapse—the system is false.

### Gravitational Hierarchy

The model claims that gravitational hierarchy is stable.  
If lower‑order Domains consistently override higher‑order Domains in conditions where the physics predict the opposite, the hierarchy is false.

### Exposure and Reorganization

The model claims that exposure forces identity reorganization.  
If betrayal, responsibility load, existential proximity, or structural failure produce no architectural change, the Domains are not real.

### Relational Asymmetry

A relational pair is the interaction between two people occupying different Identity Domains.  
The model claims that relational pairs behave according to the physics of their respective Domains.  
If cross‑Domain interactions consistently behave as same‑Domain interactions—without collapse, override, or distortion—the relational geometry is false.

### Movement Physics

The model claims that drift is slow, collapse is sudden, reconstruction is uneven, and tectonic events are irreversible.  
If identity movement consistently violates these patterns, the system breaks.

---

## Epistemic Boundaries

### Descriptive, Not Empirical

The system is grounded in structural observation, not experimental measurement.

### Stable, Not Complete

Unmodeled sub‑domains, edge cases, or developmental variants may exist.  
Their existence does not invalidate the system unless they violate the falsification conditions.

### Partial, Not Total

The system does not model neurodivergence, psychosis, addiction, severe trauma, altered states, or spiritual experience.  
These require different frameworks.

---

## Conclusion

Identity Cartography is a coherent identity‑physics system with explicit non‑claims and hard falsification conditions; it holds only while its gravitational, relational, and exposure‑based predictions remain consistent with observable human behavior.
