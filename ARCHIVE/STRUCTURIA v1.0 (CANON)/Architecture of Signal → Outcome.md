Purpose

This canon defines the minimal architecture by which inputs become outcomes.

It answers one question only:

How does something experienced turn into something attributed — without collapsing responsibility, inventing skills, or moralizing behavior?

This canon is descriptive, not prescriptive.  
It is a map, not a method.

Core Assertion

Nothing acts directly.  
Everything passes through layers.

If a concept is treated as causal without passing through the appropriate layer, the model is being violated.

The Architecture (Top → Bottom)

1. Signals

What the system detects.

Signals are:

·        emotional

·        social

·        situational

·        immediate

·        information‑bearing

Examples:

·        fear

·        urgency

·        anger

·        confidence displays

·        discomfort

·        excitement

Rules:

·        Signals carry information only

·        Signals do not decide

·        Signals do not justify action

·        Signals must be interpreted

Failure mode:

Treating signals as instructions or virtues

2. Amplifiers

What scales magnitude.

Amplifiers are:

·        infrastructural

·        non‑directional

·        morally neutral

·        always secondary

Examples:

·        intelligence

·        processing speed

·        verbal fluency

·        wit

·        working memory

·        associative range

Rules:

·        Amplifiers increase speed and scope

·        Amplifiers do not steer

·        Amplifiers cannot correct error

·        Amplifiers magnify both good and bad systems

Failure mode:

Treating amplification as competence or reliability

3. Lenses

What changes visibility.

Lenses are:

·        perceptual

·        explanatory

·        non‑decisive

·        ambiguity‑increasing

Examples:

·        systems thinking

·        abstraction

·        modeling

·        first‑principles reasoning

·        pattern recognition

Rules:

·        Lenses reveal structure

·        Lenses expand awareness

·        Lenses do not stop action

·        Lenses do not carry consequence

Failure mode:

Letting explanation replace decision

4. Mechanisms

What decides and constrains.

Mechanisms are:

·        responsibility‑bearing

·        consequence‑aware

·        scarce

·        irreversible once exercised

Examples:

·        judgment

·        restraint

·        stopping decisions

·        acceptance of cost

Rules:

·        Mechanisms terminate analysis

·        Mechanisms accept error

·        Mechanisms bind action to consequence

·        Mechanisms cannot be optimized directly

Failure mode:

Attempting to train, improve, or moralize mechanisms

5. Outcomes / Attributions

What is named after time passes.

Attributions are:

·        retrospective

·        social

·        compressive

·        fragile

Examples:

·        wisdom

·        bravery

·        composure

·        credibility

·        trust

·        authority

Rules:

·        Attributions cannot be claimed

·        Attributions cannot be trained

·        Attributions only appear after consequence

·        Attributions are lost faster than gained

Failure mode:

Treating attributions as inputs or requirements

Motion Through the System

The only valid flow is:

Signal

→ (scaled by Amplifiers)

→ (interpreted through Lenses)

→ Decision by Mechanism

→ Outcome

→ Attribution

Any shortcut violates the architecture.

Explicit Prohibitions

The following are architectural violations:

·        Signal → Action (emotion as mandate)

·        Amplifier → Decision (intelligence as authority)

·        Lens → Outcome (explanation as solution)

·        Attribution → Mechanism (wisdom as method)

·        Mechanism → Optimization (judgment as skill)

If any article implies these, it is incorrect.

Relationship to Other Canons

·        Orientation CanonPrevents misuse at the reader level

·        Architecture Canon (this document)Prevents drift at the author level

Orientation constrains interpretation.  
Architecture constrains construction.

Canonical Minimalism Rule

If a concept fits cleanly into one layer, the architecture is sufficient.  
If it requires a new layer, the concept is likely misclassified.

This rule is binding.

Canonical Warning

The architecture must not be presented wholesale to readers.

If the reader can diagram the system, the writing has failed.

The architecture is meant to be:

·        felt as inevitability

·        experienced as constraint

·        inferred through subtraction

Not taught.

Final Canonical Statement

Signals inform.  
Amplifiers scale.  
Lenses reveal.  
Mechanisms decide.  
Attributions follow.

Nothing else acts.

**Architectural Note:**  
This system assumes three sequential layers—Judgment, Integration, and Narrative. Errors arise when inputs from later layers are allowed to govern earlier ones.

That’s it.  
No moralizing. No psychology. No prescriptions.

**2. The Three Layers (Defined Precisely)**

Here is the cleanest, model‑safe articulation.

**Layer 1: Judgment (Action Selection Layer)**

**Function:** Decide what to do under constraint  
**Primary question:**

Which cost can I live with?

**Characteristics:**

- Constraint‑aware
- Determinism‑compatible
- Identity‑agnostic
- Emotion is present but bracketed
- No moral scoring
- No coherence checking

**Canon alignment:**

- Judgment
- Compass
- Roots
- Sense‑making → call
- Agency under constraint

This layer **must be fast enough to allow action**.

---

**Layer 2: Integration (Meaning Absorption Layer)**

**Function:** Absorb what the action actually cost  
**Primary question:**

What did this choice do to me / others / the field?

**Characteristics:**

- Occurs **after** action
- Emotion returns naturally
- Identity begins to update
- Regret, relief, confirmation, resistance all appear
- No re‑litigation of the decision

**Canon alignment:**

- Integration canon
- Evolution
- Roots strengthening
- Learning without rewriting history

This layer **prevents denial and repetition without reflection**.

---

**Layer 3: Narrative / Identity (Story Layer)**

**Function:** Explain the action to self and others  
**Primary question:**

What kind of person am I because of this?

**Characteristics:**

- Optional but inevitable
- Socially shaped
- Highly distortable
- Where virtue, shame, pride, justification live
- Most likely to lie

**Canon alignment:**

- Shoots
- Expression
- Styling & inference risks
- Misuse vectors

This layer **is not wrong**, but it is the least reliable.

---

**3. How These Layers Relate to the Architecture Canon**

Here’s the key insight:

**The architecture canon already assumes these layers — it just enforces boundaries between them instead of naming them.**

Let’s map them directly.

The canon already punishes violations of these boundaries:

- Identity leaking into judgment → paralysis
- Judgment leaking into integration → regret loops
- Narrative replacing integration → moral laundering

You’ve been enforcing this behaviorally.  
Now you’re naming it structurally.

**4. The Crucial Clarification You Were Circling**

You asked:

“When I step out of the model and evaluate coherence… wait, that’s going back into the model?”

No.

This is the correction that matters:

**Judgment is a selection function.**

**Coherence is an integration function.**

They are not the same layer.

The confusion only arises if:

- the model is treated as a place to live
- instead of a tool to move through

The architecture canon already assumes **movement**.  
Naming the layers makes that explicit.

---

**5. Does the Model Require Staying in Any Layer?**

No — and this is important.

- You **enter Judgment** to act
- You **exit into Integration** to absorb cost
- You **drift into Narrative** because you’re human

The failure mode is not entering Layer 3.

The failure mode is:

- using Layer 3 to redo Layer 1
- or letting Layer 3 pretend it was Layer 1

That’s where distortion happens.
