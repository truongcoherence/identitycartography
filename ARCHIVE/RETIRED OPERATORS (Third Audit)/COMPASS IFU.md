# COMPASS
### Recognizing the Situation You Are Actually In



![](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/truongcoherence/identitycartography-structuria/refs/heads/main/OPERATORS/Orientation.png)
## **Purpose**

Compass is used when:
- everything feels urgent but unclear
- interpretations conflict
- the terrain has not settled
- you cannot tell what kind of problem you are facing

The tool returns a single outcome:
**the structural condition of the situation.**

## Why This Tool Exists
Most early‑stage difficulties are not caused by:

- lack of effort
- poor planning
- unclear goals
- indecision

They are caused by uncertainty about the structure of the situation itself.

Some situations:

- are still forming
- have multiple plausible interpretations
- have already resolved without being understood
- involve multiple interacting systems
- respond to small changes
- ignore small changes

Compass exists because:

- you cannot choose the right method until you know the structure
- you cannot reason clearly while modes are competing
- you cannot force clarity before the situation stabilizes

Orientation prevents premature action by revealing the condition you are actually in.

## Conditions of Use
Compass only functions when:

- the situation is unclear
- interpretations diverge
- the moment has not yet taken shape
- you are unsure what kind of reasoning applies

Compass cannot operate when:

- the situation is already resolved
- the structure is already known
- the moment is purely interpersonal
- the problem is already defined

Compass requires:

- willingness to pause
- acceptance that clarity cannot be forced
- recognition that the situation must reveal itself

## How Compass Works
Compass classifies a moment across five structural checks:

#### 1. Stability Check

Is the situation still forming?
Would new information change what is possible?

If yes → unstable structure.

#### 2. Convergence Check

Do interpretations lead to the same constraint?
Are options still meaningfully different?

If no → collapsing structure.

#### 3. Temporal Check

Has the outcome already occurred?
Is this about something unresolved or something finished?

If finished → completed outcome.

#### 4. Structural Scope Check

Is this one structure or multiple interacting ones?
Can the situation be explained without system conflict?

If multiple → structural conflict.

#### 5. Sensitivity Check

Do small changes produce different outcomes?
Or does the system behave the same regardless?

If changes matter → responsive system.
If changes do not matter → stable system.

## Structural Resolution
Every situation resolves into one of six structural conditions:

#### A. Expanding Structure

Still forming; multiple interpretations remain valid.

#### B. Constrained Resolution

Options exist but are shaped by tradeoffs.

#### C. Completed Outcome

The event has already occurred; the structure is fixed.

#### D. Structural Conflict

Multiple systems impose incompatible constraints.

#### E. Responsive System

Changes in constraints alter outcomes.

#### F. Stable System

Changes no longer affect outcomes.

Orientation ends when the situation resolves into one of these states.

## Manual Mode
#### 1. Name the terrain
Identify what kind of situation you are in.

#### 2. Mark your position
State where you stand relative to forces and constraints.

#### 3. Identify direction of movement
Clarify what is shifting or pressuring the moment.

##### 4. Define the horizon
Articulate what “resolved” would look like.

#### 5. Choose the next step
Select the smallest action that moves toward that horizon.

Success
The map clicks; the situation becomes navigable.

Failure
You act without bearings.

## Example — Early‑Stage Biomedical Engineering Work 

Situation
A new biomedical engineer is responsible for two unstable situations:

1. Infusion Pump Rollout
- network configurations shifting
- vendor delivery schedule changing
- nursing training dates unclear
- half the pumps not yet delivered

2. Nurse Call Failure
- alarms delayed or not routing
- nursing blames workflow
- IT blames middleware
- Facilities blames wiring
- Leadership demands resolution

Every update changes the picture.
Every stakeholder provides a different interpretation.
Nothing is stable.

This is the terrain Orientation is built for.

### Compass Output

Condition: Expanding Structure

Why?
The situation is still forming.
Key details are shifting:
- pump deliveries
- network requirements
- alarm‑routing factors
- staffing
- stakeholder expectations

Interpretations conflict.
Small changes materially alter what is possible.
No single constraint has stabilized.

## What This Means

You are not choosing between tasks.
You are standing inside a situation that has not yet revealed its shape.

The uncertainty is structural, not personal.

## What Reasoning Applies

This moment requires orientation, not execution.

Your task is to:

- observe what stabilizes
- track which constraints persist
- avoid committing to a plan prematurely

#### Clarifying Points

multiple systems are interacting (clinical workflow, IT networking, vendor logistics, biomed operations)

- each system is producing valid signals
- none of the signals align
- no interpretation can yet be treated as definitive

## Where to Focus

Look for:

- what stabilizes first
- which constraints persist
- which interpretations collapse

Stop here.
The condition is clear.
No further steps apply until the structure stabilizes.

### Structural Conditions (Reference)

Compass classifies situations into:

- Expanding Structure
- Constrained Resolution
- Completed Outcome
- Structural Conflict
- Responsive System
- Stable System

Once the condition is known, the mode of reasoning becomes self‑evident.

## Failure Modes

Compass fails when:

- you force a decision before the structure stabilizes
- you treat ambiguity as personal confusion
- you collapse multiple interpretations prematurely
- you misidentify conflict where visibility is incomplete
- you apply the wrong tool to the wrong condition

#### Most common failure:
- acting before the situation has taken shape.

## Closing
Orientation does not solve the situation.
It reveals the condition you are standing in.

It shows:
- what is stable
- what is not
- what has resolved
- what has not
- where multiple meanings remain
- when acting would be premature

Compass does not remove uncertainty.
It makes uncertainty intelligible — and therefore workable.
