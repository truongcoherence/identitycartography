# Architecture of Signal and Outcome

### Public Canon, Working Draft

**Status:** Work in progress, labeled as such deliberately. See the Judgment public canon document for the full explanation of the confidence labels used throughout this series. As a quick reference:

- **Established** — more than one independently-sourced observation, checked against competing explanations, holding up.
- **Supported, verification in progress** — real observational grounding exists; formal transcription and independent re-checking are underway.
- **Provisional** — a single observation; worth taking seriously, not yet load-bearing.
- **Conceptual only** — a clearly stated idea with no linked field observation yet. Not a discarded claim — just untested so far.

**A note specific to this document:** unlike the five domain documents in this series (Judgment, Boundaries, Cohesion, Stewardship, Peril), this one describes a cross-cutting architecture — a general model of how a person moves from noticing something to acting on it to being judged for the result — rather than a single identity domain. It draws on some of the same underlying accounts already described elsewhere in this series, generalized consistently with how they appear in those documents.

---

## 1. What This Architecture Describes

A general pipeline: **Signal** (something noticed — emotional, social, situational, or immediate) → **Amplifier** (something that increases the signal's felt weight or urgency) → **Lens** (the interpretive frame applied to it) → **Mechanism** (the actual underlying process producing a decision or outcome) → **Outcome/Attribution** (what happens, and who or what gets credited or blamed for it).

Alongside the five layers, this model carries an explicit list of **Prohibitions** — specific shortcuts between layers that the model says should never be treated as valid, because skipping the layers in between produces a predictable kind of error. Two of those Prohibitions are described below with their current evidentiary status.

## 2. A Correction to How This Model Was Originally Described

This model was originally presented as deriving directly, layer for layer, from an unrelated part of this framework's own conceptual vocabulary. On closer, direct re-checking, that turned out to be true for one specific connection and overstated for another — the weaker connection has been loosened rather than left as a stronger claim than the evidence actually supports. The core five-layer structure itself is unaffected by this correction; only the claim about exactly where it came from has been adjusted.

Separately, two instructions originally attached to this model have been removed outright, because they directly conflicted with this project's own commitment to describing its methods openly rather than concealing them from readers: an instruction that the architecture should never be presented plainly enough for a reader to fully map it out, and a rule stated with more certainty than this project asks any other single claim to carry without having earned it first. Readers of this document are, on purpose, being given the same view of the model that this project's own internal work uses.

## 3. The Five-Layer Model's Prohibitions — Current Evidentiary Status

### "Intelligence as Authority" (Amplifier → Decision) — _Provisional_

Real competence or expertise in one area is mistaken for sufficient grounds to make a decision in a categorically different area, when the two are not actually equivalent.

### "Wisdom as Method" (Attribution → Mechanism) — _Provisional_

A retrospective, social judgment about someone's character or fitness is mistaken for an actual diagnosis of the decision-making process that produced an outcome.

_Grounding for both:_ A single well-documented personal account appears to underlie both of the above — not two independent touches, but one account illustrating two different Prohibitions from two different points in its own narrative. That's stated here plainly rather than presented as broader support than it actually is. Both Prohibitions are real and worth taking seriously on the strength of that account; neither is yet confirmed by a second, independent instance.

## 4. The Three-Layer Model (Judgment → Integration → Narrative) — _Provisional_

A separate, smaller model describing a three-step internal sequence: an initial reactive judgment, followed by catching and integrating a rationalization in real time, followed by a corrected narrative account of what happened.

_Grounding:_ One personal account — the same account, generalized consistently, used elsewhere in this series to source a related but distinct claim about how a specific structural constraint should be read. Using it here, for this different claim about narrative sequence, is being evaluated on its own terms rather than assumed to transfer automatically. This model was untested prior to this document; a single instance is enough to move it from untested to worth watching, not enough to canonize.

**What remains genuinely unresolved:** whether this three-layer model and the five-layer model above describe the same underlying structure from two different angles, or are two genuinely separate claims that happen to share some vocabulary. Both models received their first real supporting evidence independently of each other; neither event resolves the relationship between them, and this document doesn't claim otherwise.

## 5. New Candidate: Signal Volume Is Not Proportional to Signal Value — _Provisional_

A signal's value doesn't increase in a straight line with how often it's sent. Past a certain point, additional signal stops adding useful information to the receiving party and starts costing their attention instead — meaning that generic advice to simply "communicate more" isn't cost-free, and can itself become a burden past a real threshold.

_Grounding:_ One workplace account — someone asking how frequently they should check in with a project stakeholder, and a reasoned answer about why more frequent contact doesn't make the other party move faster past a certain point. Held as a candidate refinement to the existing model rather than a confirmed addition; worth watching for a second instance before being treated as more than that.
