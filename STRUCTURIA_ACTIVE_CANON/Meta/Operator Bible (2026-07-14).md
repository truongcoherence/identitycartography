# Operator Bible

### Public Canon, Working Draft

**Status:** Work in progress, labeled as such deliberately. See the Judgment public canon document for the full explanation of the confidence labels used throughout this series. As a quick reference:

- **Established** — more than one independently-sourced observation, checked against competing explanations, holding up.
- **Supported, verification in progress** — real observational grounding exists; formal transcription and independent re-checking are underway.
- **Provisional** — a single observation; worth taking seriously, not yet load-bearing.
- **Conceptual only** — a clearly stated idea with no linked field observation yet. Not a discarded claim — just untested so far.

**A note specific to this document:** operators describe recurring _tools_ a person can apply to a situation — ways of noticing or interpreting what's happening — rather than domain-level laws the way the five identity documents in this series do. This document has historically been the least battle-tested part of the framework, and that's reflected honestly in the labels below: some items here are better-evidenced than almost anything else in this series, and one specific item is currently the weakest-evidenced claim published anywhere in it.

---

## 1. What Operators Are

An operator is a repeatable way of engaging with a situation — noticing something, interpreting it, or regulating a response to it — rather than a fixed trait or a domain-specific law. The same operator can, in principle, be used across more than one identity domain.

---

## 2. The Six Core Operators

Six operators currently make up the core, published set: **Lens**, **Interpreter**, **Relief**, **Seismograph**, **Probe**, and **Compass**.

**Interpreter and Lens — _Supported, verification in progress_.** These two have a genuinely different, and stronger, kind of support than most other claims in this document: direct confirmation from the actual working material that produced them, rather than from a personal case narrative. A specific structural split in how situations get read — roughly, a more rule-based mode versus a more probability-based mode — was directly verified as the origin of Lens as an operator.

**Relief, Seismograph, Probe, and Compass — _Conceptual only_.** These four are not resting on a foundation that's since been shown faulty (an earlier, broader theoretical layer this document once leaned on has been retired elsewhere in this framework), but not resting on a bad foundation is different from resting on a demonstrated good one. No dedicated positive evidence for these four specifically has been logged yet, and this document says so directly rather than letting the absence of a known problem read as the presence of confirmation.

---

## 3. Compass — A Resolved Internal Contradiction

An earlier version of this document described Compass in two incompatible ways at once — as a single, universal operator above the other five, and separately as one of six equal, domain-paired peers. That contradiction is resolved, not smoothed over: Compass is a meta-operator, not a domain-specific one, and its earlier six-way listing reflected a different, unrelated fact (that it exists as one of six actually published tools) rather than genuine functional equivalence to the other five. It doesn't pair more closely with any single identity domain than with any other.

## 4. The Five Identity-Signal Operators

A separate, smaller category — signals that something in a specific identity domain has been activated, arriving before any conscious diagnosis of what's happening. These are explicitly not the same category as a felt, automatic primitive (see the Nature of Invariants, Primitives, and Distortions document for that distinction) — they're closer to a noticing than a doing.

**Currently _Provisional_, not _Established_, for all five** — the phenomenology described for each is specific and consistent, but the entire supporting basis right now is direct, reflective self-report rather than a documented behavioral incident. That's a real form of evidence, worth taking seriously, but it's thinner than a field case with an actual event attached to it, and this document says so rather than implying the confidence a "confirmed" label would suggest elsewhere in this series.

- A feeling of significant uncertainty, recognized directly as a decision-quality moment.
- A feeling of something pushing into one's own space, physical or social.
- A feeling of being pulled apart or pressed between competing parties — distinct from the previous one, which is about intrusion into one's own space rather than pressure from multiple directions at once.
- A feeling best described as "I've been doing this for too long."
- A feeling of vulnerability, of a situation coming apart.

The specific names currently used for these five are explicitly considered placeholders, not finalized — better names likely exist, and this is a known, low-urgency open task rather than a settled naming convention.

**A related usage note, worth including here:** noticing one of these five signals doesn't obligate an immediate attempt to diagnose the full situation. Stabilizing an overwhelmed state first, before trying to identify what's actually known and unknown, is frequently the correct order rather than a failure to use the signal properly.

## 5. Still Open — How Operators Pair With Identity Domains

Beyond Compass's resolved case above, several other operator-to-domain pairings remain genuinely unresolved rather than confidently assigned. One pairing holds up reasonably well on its own logic; three others are currently weaker than earlier material presented them as being, and at least one operator's stated function appears to fit a different domain better than the one it's currently paired with. None of these has been reassigned — they're flagged for the same kind of direct, careful evaluation that resolved Compass, rather than either accepted as-is or quietly changed.

## 6. Retained, Lower Priority

A smaller set of sections describing internal mechanics and general distinctions (what an operator is _not_, how operators relate to other categories in this framework) are carried forward unchanged. These were already self-described, in an earlier version of this document, as lower-confidence and adjacent to the main canon rather than fully part of it — that status hasn't changed, and there's no urgency attached to resolving it ahead of the items above.

---

## 7. A Known Gap, Stated Directly

A significant structural distinction that existed in earlier material — describing whether a person is acting on their environment or their environment is acting on them — is not currently present in this document at all. If it turns out to be as significant as earlier material suggested, it needs to be located and formally reinstated before it can be evaluated the way everything else in this document has been. Right now, it's simply missing, and that's stated plainly rather than left for a reader to notice on their own.
