# Structuria — Peril (2026-07-14)

### Public Canon, Working Draft

**Status:** Work in progress, labeled as such deliberately. See the Judgment public canon document for the full explanation of the confidence labels used throughout this series. As a quick reference:

- **Established** — more than one independently-sourced observation, checked against competing explanations, holding up.
- **Supported, verification in progress** — real observational grounding exists; formal transcription and independent re-checking are underway.
- **Provisional** — a single observation; worth taking seriously, not yet load-bearing.
- **Conceptual only** — a clearly stated idea with no linked field observation yet. Not a discarded claim — just untested so far.

A claim that sounded coherent but was later found to have never actually been derived from real observation does exist elsewhere in this project's history — where that happened, the claim was retired outright, not published in any form. Nothing below is in that category.

**A note specific to this document:** Peril is the newest domain in this framework and, by its own nature, deals with the highest-stakes material in the corpus — situations where something genuinely irreversible happened or nearly happened. The source material behind this domain has been generalized more heavily than in the other four public documents in this series, precisely because the underlying cases are the most personally significant ones on record. Where a detail has been left out below, it's been left out on purpose.

---

## 1. What Peril Is

Peril describes what happens when a protective structure that was quietly doing real work — insulating someone from full exposure to a risk — fails, and contact with that risk is made directly for the first time. It's specifically about the failure of insulation, not about risk or hardship in general.

---

## 2. Invariants of Peril

### a. Insulation Fails Before Contact Registers as Peril — _Supported, verification in progress_

A protective structure has to actually fail — not just feel thin or theoretical — before genuine peril registers. Anticipating a risk is not the same experience as the insulation against it actually giving way.

_Grounding:_ A well-documented personal account involving a real protective structure (an institutional safeguard) that was withdrawn for a stated administrative reason, unrelated to any personal shortcoming, followed by a loss of control once contact was made — plus two further cases, one of which specifically tests the boundary by showing insulation that was tested and held rather than failed. The most detailed account already explicitly names and directly addresses the most obvious competing explanation (that the outcome reflected a personal failing rather than a structural one) rather than ignoring it — a meaningfully stronger form of support than most claims in this series currently have, even though full independent re-verification is still in progress.

### b. Revelation Attaches to Whichever Party's Insulation Failed — _Supported, verification in progress_

When contact is made, the resulting exposure and its consequences land on whichever party's protective structure actually gave way — not necessarily on whoever might seem, from the outside, most responsible or most affected.

_Grounding:_ Two personal accounts — one involving the disclosure of a significant personal truth to a close family member, with a complicated emotional aftermath; one involving the end of a close friendship following a major, unrelated change in the other person's life circumstances — plus the same account described under invariant (a). This is currently the highest-exposure claim in this entire published series, given how personal its source material is, and has been generalized accordingly.

### c. Once Contact Is Made, the Outcome Exceeds Any Single Party's Control — _Supported, verification in progress_

Once insulation has genuinely failed and contact is made, what happens next is no longer fully within any one party's control to determine or contain.

_Grounding:_ The same two primary accounts described under invariants (a) and (b), plus a secondary personal health-related account explicitly held at lower evidentiary weight, pending outside validation, rather than treated as equal-strength support.

---

## 3. Constraint of Peril

### Peril Is Not the Decision — _Supported, verification in progress_

Peril describes what happens to someone when insulation fails and contact is made — it is not itself a decision that party makes, and shouldn't be read as implying one.

_Grounding:_ The same two personal accounts described under invariant (b) above.

---

## 4. Section Currently Held Open, Pending Further Testing

A further question — whether genuinely anticipating an unavoidable exposure can itself count as a form of peril, even before any actual contact occurs — is currently being tested against two workplace-related accounts, neither of which is yet treated as a confirmed instance. This is deliberately held open rather than folded into the invariants above until the specific required test has actually been run.

## 5. Primitive of Peril

**None currently proposed.** An earlier candidate was found to have been written in the wrong structural format for a primitive to begin with, rather than failing on its underlying content. No replacement is proposed at this time.

---

## 6. Structural Distortions of Peril

**Quiet as Safety** — An outwardly calm surface is mistaken for the actual absence of risk underneath it. Unlike the other distortion in this domain, this one currently has a real, if limited, point of contact with field evidence: one account on record shows this distortion being correctly _resisted_ rather than fallen for — a person recognizing that a calm exterior didn't mean the underlying situation was actually safe. That's weaker evidence than a case that confirms the distortion by someone falling for it, and is labeled as such — _Provisional_, not _Established_.

**Probability as Certainty** — A likely outcome is mistaken for a guaranteed one. Currently _Conceptual only_ — no dedicated field case yet ties this distortion to a real account.

## 7. A Note on How to Read This Document

As with the other documents in this series, labels here will change as more material is checked — some claims will move toward _Established_, and it's possible some won't hold up and will be revised or retired instead. Peril in particular is expected to change the most over time, simply because it's the newest and least battle-tested domain in the framework, and its source material is the hardest to fully verify without asking more of the people involved than this project is willing to ask. This document will be updated as that work continues.
