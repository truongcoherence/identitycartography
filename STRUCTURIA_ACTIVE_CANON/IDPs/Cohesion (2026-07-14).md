# Cohesion

### Public Canon, Working Draft

**Status:** Work in progress, labeled as such deliberately. See the Judgment public canon document for the full explanation of the confidence labels used throughout this series. As a quick reference:

- **Established** — more than one independently-sourced observation, checked against competing explanations, holding up.
- **Supported, verification in progress** — real observational grounding exists; formal transcription and independent re-checking are underway.
- **Provisional** — a single observation; worth taking seriously, not yet load-bearing.
- **Conceptual only** — a clearly stated idea with no linked field observation yet. Not a discarded claim — just untested so far, which is common for a newer part of the framework.

A claim that sounded coherent but was later found to have never actually been derived from real observation does exist elsewhere in this project's history — where that happened, the claim was retired outright, not published in any form. Nothing below is in that category.

---

## 1. What Cohesion Is

Cohesion is what allows separate parts of a system — separate people, separate roles, separate institutional functions — to hold together as one working structure rather than pulling apart under their own internal logic. It depends on the parts actually needing each other structurally, not just cooperating by preference.

---

## 2. Invariants of Cohesion

### a. Cohesion Requires Structural Interdependence — _Supported, verification in progress_

Cohesion isn't produced by goodwill or shared intention alone — it requires the parts of a system to genuinely depend on one another in a way that would actually break something if removed.

_Grounding:_ Two independently-sourced cases across different institutional coordination contexts. Full case transcription and independent re-verification are in progress; there isn't yet enough written detail on record to move this claim further than its current label.

### b. Cohesion Rests on a Shared Frame — _Supported, verification in progress_

Two parties can each hold a model of a situation that is internally coherent on its own terms and still be fundamentally incompatible with each other's model — without either party being at fault for the mismatch.

_Grounding:_ Two cases, reused from elsewhere in this framework where they source a related but distinct claim about capability and constraint. Their use here, for this specific claim about a shared frame, is being checked on its own terms rather than assumed to carry over automatically just because the underlying incidents already hold up for a different purpose.

### c. Convergence Point — _Supported, verification in progress_

A single person can end up structurally positioned where multiple independently legitimate requirements collide — not because anyone made an error, but because no existing mechanism synthesizes the competing demands before they land on that one person.

_Grounding:_ Three cases, the most detailed of which involves an equipment planner caught between two separate scheduling priorities, each fully valid within its own originating context. This is currently the best-supported claim in this document that hasn't yet reached the _Established_ label — the underlying account holds up well on close reading, but hasn't yet been transcribed into a standalone, independently re-checkable file.

## 3. Section Currently Held Open, Pending a Specific Test

A working hypothesis about how a system distributes load across its weaker connection points is currently being tested against a specific, predetermined falsification condition rather than being canonized on the strength of its early supporting cases. Two prior versions of this idea were found to directly contradict available evidence as originally worded, which is why this version is being held rather than promoted on the same timeline as the invariants above. This is intentional caution, not a stalled idea — the required test is defined and pending, not indefinitely deferred.

## 4. Structural Distortions of Cohesion

**Harmony as Health** — The absence of visible conflict is mistaken for the presence of genuine structural cohesion. A system can be quiet because it's healthy, or quiet because something load-bearing has already broken and nothing is left to strain against.

**Closeness as Commitment** — Emotional or relational closeness is mistaken for structural interdependence. Two people or roles can feel close while depending on each other for nothing that would actually break if the relationship ended.

_A note on this section's status:_ as with the other domains in this series, these two distortions are conceptually well-formed but not yet tied to a dedicated field case of their own.
