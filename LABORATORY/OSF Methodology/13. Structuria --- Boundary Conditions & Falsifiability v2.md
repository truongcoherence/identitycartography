# Boundary Conditions & Falsifiability

**Status:** July 12, 2026. Revises `Boundary_Conditions___Falsifiability.md`. The original document's falsifiability posture was never retired — it was flagged as sound in principle but never tested against a real case at the time of drafting. This version keeps what held up, retires or rescopes what didn't survive the restructuring, and organizes falsifiability as it actually now operates: at three distinct levels that were never previously related to each other.

---

## 1. Four Levels of Falsifiability, Now Explicit

This project did not end up with one falsification method. It ended up with four, operating at different grains, developed at different times, never previously fully connected:

- **Construct-level** — the five-test battery (Substitution, Independent Derivation, Circularity, Compression, Adversarial Reframe). Tests whether a term or category is doing real structural work at all.

- **Invariant-level** — the Pattern Comparison Protocol's Outcome D. Tests whether a _specific_ invariant is contradicted by a specific case, with immediate retirement if the contradiction holds (precedent: Stewardship's Custodial Authority).

- **Cross-domain-dynamics-level** — the named falsifiers below, inherited from the original document. Test broad claims about how domains interact with each other, not any single invariant.

- **Operator-level** — new, July 12, 2026, developed in direct response to a question about what would falsify an operator specifically. Tests whether a claimed detection signal (a felt operator like Arbiter or Gate) reliably corresponds to the structural condition it claims to indicate. Distinct from the other three because operators are correspondence claims (signal → structural condition), not structural claims about a domain itself.

None of these substitute for the others. A construct can pass Substitution and still have an invariant under it that gets contradicted later. Keeping all four, explicitly related for the first time.

**A related, plain-language companion to construct-level Substitution, named July 12, 2026: does the case survive substituting out the specific person in it?** Substitution asks whether a _term_ is doing real work by testing whether another word can replace it without loss. This asks the same question about identity: if a case's validity holds up identically after replacing the specific person with a generic role — "the subject" instead of a named individual — that's real evidence the claim was about the configuration all along, not about anything particular to who happened to be in it. It cuts both ways, and the more useful direction is the negative one: if a case quietly loses persuasive force once the specific person is removed, that's a sign the "structural" reading was borrowing credibility from identity rather than standing on the pattern alone. Confirmed once already, on CASE-028 — every element supporting its invariants held identically under the substitution.

### 1.1 Operator Falsifiers, in Full

An operator claims a felt signal reliably indicates a specific structural condition. Four distinct ways this can fail, not one:

1. **False positive** — the signal fires, but the claimed condition isn't actually present; the same felt signal turns out to indicate something else entirely, or nothing structural at all.

2. **False negative** — the structural condition is genuinely present, but the signal doesn't fire, with no discernible pattern to the misses. A detector that only sometimes detects isn't reliable.

3. **Non-structural correlation** — the deepest failure. The signal turns out to track something purely psychological (a general anxiety trait, say) rather than any actual structural condition — present regardless of whether the claimed condition exists at all. This doesn't just falsify one operator; it's Signal as Judgment happening to the operator theory itself, and it's the failure mode most worth checking for specifically, not the easiest one to dismiss. **Practical test, added July 12, 2026, naturalistic rather than manufactured:** does the signal recur around a specific, repeating _shape_ of situation, or does it fire regardless of situation? Recurrence tied to a consistent situational pattern is real diagnostic evidence for a structural signal. Recurrence independent of situation — the same feeling showing up whether or not the claimed structural condition is actually present — is evidence for this falsifier, not against it. This is the naturalistic version of a test that would otherwise require a manufactured experiment, which this project has already ruled out as introducing its own distortion.

4. **Utility failure — a separate claim from the diagnostic ones above, and not a competition between two exclusive first moves.** The original framing of this falsifier was too binary: addressing the feeling versus addressing the structure, as though only one could go first. In practice they're often sequential, not competing — regulating an overstimulated state before structural diagnosis is even possible is not a failure of the operator theory, it's frequently the correct order of operations. The real test is narrower: once regulation has occurred, does the structural identification (what's known, what's unknown, for an Arbiter event) add something reassurance alone would not have? If addressing the structure afterward produces no better outcome than stopping at regulation, the operator's practical value is falsified independent of whether its diagnosis was accurate — but the test has to respect the sequence, not force a false choice at the moment the signal first fires.

## 2. Non-Claims — Retained, Substance Intact

Not Typology, Not Behavioral Prediction, Not Cultural Modeling, Not Clinical, Not Ethical — all five still hold and remain consistent with everything built since, particularly Law 01 and Judgment's Not Blame constraint, which are direct expressions of Not Ethical and Not Clinical specifically. Retained as originally stated, with one note: the original's vocabulary ("gravitational pull, override, interference, collapse") is drawn from the Intercontinental Physics material, most of which remains untested aether. The _claims_ in this section don't depend on that vocabulary being validated — they'd hold under plainer language just as well — but the phrasing should eventually be updated to stop borrowing confidence from a still-unproven layer.

## 3. Cross-Domain Falsifiers — Reassessed Individually

**Four of five retired, July 12, 2026, per author decision: written when the IDPs were still continents, not yet independently tested since. Reinstatement condition, stated once, applying to all four: if a future case independently re-derives the underlying concept on its own terms, under current definitions, it can be reinstated then — not kept alive now on the strength of vocabulary from a framework this project has already moved past.**

**Single-Domain Governance — RETIRED.** Assumed domains are mutually exclusive occupancy states requiring "override" to coexist. Already contradicted by current practice, not merely outdated: multiple domains routinely operate concurrently on the same case (the CT/Engineering case alone sources Judgment, Boundaries, and Cohesion at once, with no collapse or override). Not a falsifier still waiting to be tested — one that's already failed quietly under the current model.

**Gravitational Hierarchy — RETIRED.** Tested Intercontinental Physics' hierarchy claim, itself still-untested aether-adjacent material built on the continent framework. One candidate case existed (the diagnosis/isolation case) but was never formally run before the reinstatement condition above takes precedence.

**Exposure and Reorganization — Retained, as revised.** Not part of this retirement — tied directly to Peril, an active current domain, not continent-era vocabulary. Stands as previously updated: existential proximity as one example trigger among several, not a requirement.

**Relational Asymmetry — RETIRED.** Built around "relational pairs," a continent-model concept never actively used anywhere in this session's restoration of the current five IDPs.

**Movement Physics — RETIRED.** Tested _The Three Modes of Movement_, itself still untested as formal canon and built on the same continent-era framing as the other three.

## 4. Epistemic Boundaries — One Real Correction

**"Descriptive, Not Empirical" no longer accurately describes this project and should be revised, not retained as written.** This was true of the original canon. It is not true of the corpus this session built: the Evidentiary Tier System grades evidence by source and strength, the Case Validity Checklist and Pattern Comparison Protocol exist specifically to test claims against real cases, and Outcome D provides for actual retirement on contradiction. That's empirical grounding — not experimental, no controlled trials, and deliberately not: manufactured test scenarios were explicitly rejected as introducing their own distortion. The accurate description is **naturalistic and empirical, not experimental** — a real, specific, defensible epistemic position, not the same claim the original document made.

"Stable, Not Complete" and "Partial, Not Total" — both retained without change; neither has been challenged by anything in this reconstruction.

## 5. Conclusion — Revised

Identity Cartography is falsifiable at three distinct levels — construct, invariant, and cross-domain dynamics — each with its own tested or partially-tested mechanism, related here explicitly for the first time. It holds only as long as claims at all three levels remain consistent with actual gathered evidence, naturalistic rather than experimental, with retirement as a real and already-exercised outcome, not a theoretical one.
