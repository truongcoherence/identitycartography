# Structuria — The Full Pipeline: Process and Checks

**Status:** July 19, 2026. A single map of the entire process, from a raw observation to a public claim, showing which document governs each stage and why the checks exist in this order. No individual methodology document was designed to do this job — each one governs a single transition. This one exists to show how they connect.

---

## The Pipeline, Stage by Stage

### **1. Raw observation → field note.** An event happens, or is recalled, and gets written down for the first time.

#### **Governing document**: none 

This is a deliberate, acknowledged limit, not a gap. Nothing can check whether a contemporaneous note accurately reflects what actually happened; there is no earlier artifact to check it against. The only real check available at this layer is independent external corroboration, not a better internal document — see the Limitations and Invitation to Extend document, which states this plainly rather than pretending an internal fix exists.

### **2. Field note → validated candidate.** Deciding whether an observation is even eligible to become a source case.

#### **Governing document**: Case Validity Checklist

Structural validity gate (real, dated, specific, tested against all five domains and the Meta-Concept Layer, survives adversarial reframe), exposure and consent gate, batch discipline against cherry-picking, and requiring every element to clear on the written record alone, not on unstated memory.

For third party observations, please see the Story Corp Ingestion Policy.

For comparative analysis between publicized case studies, please see the New Source Ingestion Criteria.

### **3. Validated candidate → case file.** Turning an accepted observation into a properly structured record.

#### **Governing documents**: the Case Note Processing Standard, its transcription-fidelity addendum, the Specificity Check, and the Reconstruction Distortion Risk document.

The addendum keeps a drafted narrative checkable against its raw source rather than trusted because it reads well. The Specificity Check catches the specific failure mode of a detail being both unusually precise and suspiciously convenient for the claim it supports. The Reconstruction Distortion Risk document governs the harder case — no raw note exists at all — and requires labeling the result honestly as retrospective reconstruction, sequenced (recall first, scrutinize after) to avoid the check contaminating the recall itself.

For comparative analysis between publicized case studies, please see Comparative Constraint Audit.

### **4. Case file → classification.** Deciding what a validated case actually does to existing canon.

#### **Governing document**:  Pattern Comparison Protocol

Tested against each invariant individually, not the domain as a whole; Compression and Independent Derivation run before anything is accepted; competing domain readings must be named and explicitly defeated, not just quietly outcompeted; ambiguous cross-domain fits get the Substitution test run between the two candidates directly. Four possible outcomes — confirms, refines, proposes something new (held, not canonized, until a second independent source), or contradicts (immediate retirement if the contradiction holds). §5 additionally guards against the mirror-image of blame: using a structural finding to manufacture unwarranted discredit on a positive outcome.

### **5. Classification → canon text.** Writing what was found into the actual domain document.

#### **Governing document**: Pattern Comparison Protocol

The newest check in this chain, added after this exact transition was found unwatched — every other stage had something checking it; this one didn't, until Boundaries invariant (e) was found describing itself as "quadruple-sourced" when two of its four citations were reused from invariant (d). Requires tracing every cited Case ID against its other citations before finalizing a multi-sourced claim's language.

### **6. Canon → public form.** Deciding what, if anything, can safely leave the internal record.

#### **Governing document**: Publication & Anonymization Standard

The Recognizability Test, the tiered transformation techniques (paraphrase, generalize, composite, exclude), the baseline defaults (subject-substitution, degendering, confirmed at zero evidentiary cost twice), the reversal-key rule (a record of how something was anonymized must never travel with the anonymized content itself), and the k-anonymity limit — a label only protects to the extent other plausible candidates exist behind it, which this corpus, drawn from one life, currently cannot fully supply on its own.

### **7. Public form → public output.** What actually gets published, and under what standing commitments.

#### **Governing documents**: (see below)

The Pedigree and Development History, the Limitations and Invitation to Extend, The Current Events (Tier 3) Corroboration Policy, and, for any StoryCorps-derived material specifically, the Ingestion Policy's separate publication gate — a commitment made directly to an outside party, distinct from and stricter than this project's own internal publication clearance.

## Two Grading Systems That Run Across Every Stage, Not Within One

**The Evidentiary Tier System** grades how much a piece of evidence is worth, independent of where in the pipeline it currently sits — the author's own dated observation outweighs elicited or secondhand material, which outweighs general external corroboration, which can never establish a claim alone.

**The four-level Falsifiability framework** (construct, invariant, cross-domain dynamics, operator) states what would have to be true for a claim at any level to be shown false, and exists independently of any single stage above — it's a standing property every claim has to carry, checked whenever a claim is examined, not only when it's first produced.

---

## What This Document Does Not Do

It does not replace any of the documents it summarizes — each one is more detailed and more precise than its entry here. This exists to answer a different question: given everything built, where does a given check actually sit, and what does it leave for the next stage to catch. If a future gap gets found the way canon-integration fidelity was, it belongs here as a new stage, not only in the document that fixes it.

## Addendum, July 14, 2026 — Where the Meta-Structural Documents Actually Sit

Four documents describe properties of the whole system rather than a single domain: the Meta-Concept Layer, the Architecture of Signal and Outcome, the Operator Bible, and Nature of Invariants, Primitives, and Distortions. None were named anywhere in the pipeline above. On inspection, they don't need the same fix — three genuinely different situations, not one.

**Already inside the case pipeline, just never named as a legitimate target of it: the Meta-Concept Layer and the Architecture of Signal and Outcome.** Both have already received real classification traffic through the Pattern Comparison Protocol, run exactly like a domain claim would be — CASE-043 and CASE-044 processed against the Architecture document's own Signal → Action prohibition; CASE-046 processed against the Meta-Concept Layer with a documented defeated alternative, held per Outcome C precisely like any domain candidate. The mechanism was never broken. The Protocol's own language simply assumed "domain" meant one of the five IDPs. Corrected: Stage 4 and Stage 5 (classification, and classification-to-canon-text fidelity) apply equally to these two documents, not only to Judgment through Peril.

**A genuinely separate track, correctly not folded into the case pipeline: the Operator Bible.** Everything confirmed in it — the five Identity Operators, Compass's resolved status, both of Boundaries' retired primitives — came from direct interrogation of the author, not from a case moving through the Checklist and Protocol. This is a real, distinct validation method, parallel to the case pipeline rather than a stage within it, and deserves to be documented as such rather than assumed to work the same way.

**A different kind of gap, solved by neither of the above: Nature of Invariants, Primitives, and Distortions.** This document isn't a claim awaiting classification — it's a running aggregate of claims already classified elsewhere ("eleven of twelve distortions untested, one exception"). That count goes stale the moment any domain's Stage 5 or 6 touches a primitive, invariant, or distortion, unless this document is reopened and updated at the same time. Required addition: whenever canon integration (Stage 5) changes a domain's primitive, invariant, or distortion status, this document's aggregate counts must be checked and updated in the same pass — not treated as a separate task to remember later.
