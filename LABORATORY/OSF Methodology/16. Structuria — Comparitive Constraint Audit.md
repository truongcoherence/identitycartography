# Comparative Constraint Audit Protocol — Working Standard (Draft) 

**Status:** Proposed July 15, 2026. 
**Purpose:** Governs the process of analyzing raw narratives against systemic constraints and physics-based logic, serving as an audit against domain-specific narrative bias.

## 0. The Logic of the Audit 

This protocol formalizes the shift from "observation-to-interpretation" to "observation-to-multiple-analyses." It recognizes that domain-specific expertise often functions as a narrative filter, masking mechanical reality with jargon. 

This audit forces the analyst to strip away that filter and examine the raw structural geometry of the event. The audit operates on the principle that if a phenomenon is truly systemic, its mechanical logic should hold
independent of the domain's "official" explanation.

## 1. The Four-Step Execution Sequence

Cases are to be processed through the following sequence in strict order. No step may be bypassed, and the findings of each must be documented separately. 

### 1.1 — Narrative Extraction (Raw Data) 

Read the source material in its entirety. Extract only the objective facts:

- the timeline
- the actors
- the environmental conditions
- and the documented constraints.

**Prohibition:** Do not include conclusions, "lessons learned," or expert interpretations from the source. 

**Output:** A distilled, chronological Fact Log. 
### 1.2 — Constraint Mapping (TheCartography Phase) 


Map the structural boundaries that existed *before* the action was taken.

Use Identity Cartography’s existing framework to identify the environment’s configuration. * 

**Task:** Identify the "false choices" versus the "possible paths." 

**Focus:** Define the information architecture:
- *Where was the signal, and where was the noise?

### 1.3 — Physical & Mechanical Analysis (The Purity Phase) 

Analyze the mapped constraints against the physics of the environment.

**Criterion:** Ignore human variables (fear, ego, fatigue, "willpower") and focus on the
mechanical limit. Given the constraints in 1.2, what was the physical or information-flow
probability of the outcome? 

**Test:** Does the action taken represent a failure of the agent, or a failure of the architecture? 
### 1.4 — The Confrontation (Control Phase) 

Compare your mechanical findings against the provided "official" explanation from the source domain. 

**If matched:** The explanation is corroborated by structural analysis. 

**If mismatched:** Document the divergence. The mismatch itself is the primary finding. It identifies where the domain's narrative bias obscures the mechanical reality.

## 2. Integrity Safeguards

### 2.1 — Defeated Alternative Requirement 

If your structural analysis diverges from the official domain narrative, you must explicitly document the official explanation and defeat it. 

**Format:** "The domain attributes this to [X]; however, mechanical analysis shows [Y], specifically because [mechanical reason]." 

**Mandatory:** Silently passing over a provided explanation is forbidden.

### 2.2 — Independent Derivation Check

Would a second analyst, having access only to the raw Fact Log (1.1) and the framework definitions, reach the same conclusion regarding the mechanical failure? If not, the mapping in 1.2 requires further refinement for clarity, not further persuasion.

### 2.3 — The "Slush" Prevention Check 

If this case, when analyzed, causes existing invariants to feel uncertain or "slushy," do not force a resolution to maintain canon
stability. 

**Action:** Flag the concept as "Under Review: Comparative Divergence." 

**Persistence:** The concept remains in this status until a secondary case corroborates the
mechanical audit.

## 3. Scope and Application 

This protocol is the primary tool for auditing
historical case studies (aviation, military, economics, business) without requiring prior domain expertise. 

It is a "beginner’s mind" diagnostic filter designed to identify systemic bias that is
invisible to industry insiders.
