# Structuria Evidentiary Tier System — Working Standard

**Status:** Formalized July 12, 2026, from working practice established during the Judgment/Cohesion/Boundaries/Stewardship restoration sessions. Governs all future canon restoration and Tier 3 corroboration work.

---

## Rationale

Restoration work requires source evidence of varying strength. Rather than treating all evidence as equivalent, or discarding weaker evidence entirely, this system ranks data by evidentiary weight and specifies how each tier may legitimately be used. The core principle: **weaker-tier evidence may corroborate generality, but may never substitute for or outweigh stronger-tier evidence, and must always be labeled by tier wherever it appears in canon or provenance notes.**

This system was developed in direct response to a methodological risk identified during restoration: cued elicitation (asking the author for a story matching a hypothesis already in mind) risks producing confirmation bias rather than genuine derivation. The tier system, combined with the uncued raw-corpus check and the Tier 3 generality check described below, is the corrective process developed to guard against that risk.

---

## The Tiers

### Tier 1 — Third-Party Researcher Observation

**Definition:** Direct observation of a structural pattern by a researcher external to the author, independent of the author's own account or interpretation. **Current status:** Not yet available. No Tier 1 evidence exists anywhere in the current canon as of this document's creation. This is explicitly acknowledged as the current limitation of the entire framework — the canon is presently built without its intended strongest evidentiary layer. **Function once available:** Would represent the strongest possible confirmation of any invariant, constraint, or pattern — capable of testing claims without the author's own interpretive frame present at all. **Why the canon is being built without it first:** Per the author's own reasoning, third-party researchers require a sufficiently substantiated reference framework before they can meaningfully engage with or test it. Tier 2 material is therefore being used, deliberately and provisionally, to construct scaffolding that Tier 1 evidence can eventually be tested against — not as a permanent substitute for it.

### Tier 2 — Author's Own Field Observation

**Definition:** The author's own direct, lived experience — personal or workplace — documented as a field note or recalled directly during restoration. **Subtypes, in descending order of evidentiary strength:**

- **Uncued, raw, predating the hypothesis being tested** (e.g., field notes written before a domain's restoration began, discovered afterward to independently confirm a pattern) — strongest form of Tier 2 evidence, since it cannot have been shaped by the hypothesis it corroborates.
- **Cued, but independently verified against raw corpus afterward** — legitimate once cross-checked, as was done for Cohesion and Judgment.
- **Cued and not yet cross-checked** — should be explicitly flagged as such in provenance notes (e.g., "single-instance, pending") rather than presented as equivalent to verified material.
- **Self-referential** (the canon or restoration process itself as the evidentiary object, as used for Stewardship invariant (c)) — a distinct and unusual subtype. Legitimate but should always be flagged as self-referential and should not be considered as strong as an external Tier 2 case until corroborated by one. **Current status:** The primary evidentiary backbone of all restoration work completed to date (Judgment, Cohesion, Boundaries, Stewardship).

### Tier 3 — National/Institutional News and Public Reporting

**Definition:** Reporting on events, decisions, or findings at national or large-institutional scale — political, economic, scientific, or social — accessed via search and pre-digested into structural summary rather than read raw by the author. **Function:** Generality check only. Tier 3 evidence may confirm that a pattern already sourced at Tier 2 generalizes beyond the author's personal and workplace context. It may never be used to establish a new invariant, constraint, or pattern on its own, and must always be explicitly labeled "Tier 3, generality check only, not independently weighted" wherever logged. **Special handling requirement:** Per the author's explicit request, Tier 3 material is retrieved and pre-digested by the assistant rather than read directly by the author, specifically to avoid the distress of direct news exposure. Searches should be filtered by structural mechanism relevant to the open question, not by raw topic, and should exclude graphic, violent, or purely inflammatory content where a structurally relevant summary can be extracted without it. Political material should be avoided by default in favor of economic, scientific, or social domains unless political material is specifically requested, given the author's stated preference. **Known limitation:** Tier 3 sources are frequently persuasive documents (corporate rationale, post-incident analysis) rather than neutral observation, and may reflect retrospective-attribution bias in their own right (cf. the pseudoskills series' caution about retrospective leadership narratives). This should be weighed when assessing how much a Tier 3 case actually corroborates versus how much it merely restates a favorable narrative.

### Tier 4 — Historical Example (proposed, not yet in use)

**Definition:** Documented historical events, further removed in time than current Tier 3 material, potentially useful for testing whether a pattern holds across different institutional and technological contexts. **Current status:** Flagged by the author as worth considering in the future. Not yet formally defined or used in any restoration to date. Should be formally scoped (how far back, what sourcing standard, what corroboration role relative to Tier 3) before first use.

## Standing Rules for Application

1. Every piece of evidence logged in any provenance table must be tier-labeled.
2. Tier 3 (and future Tier 4) evidence must never be counted toward "confirming" an invariant on its own — only toward noting that a Tier 1/2-sourced pattern generalizes.
3. When Tier 3 evidence is the _only_ evidence available for a claim (as briefly true for parts of Boundaries' invariant (c) before further work), the claim must be explicitly marked as still requiring genuine Tier 1/2 sourcing, not treated as resolved.
4. Reused evidence (the same Tier 3 case applied to test more than one domain in a single session, as occurred with the housing bill and reproducibility-crisis cases) must be flagged as reused, not double-counted as independent corroboration across domains.
5. This tier system itself is subject to revision as Tier 1 evidence becomes available and as Tier 4 is formally defined.
