# Story Intake Analysis

**Governing documents:** Structuria Case Validity Checklist, Structuria Pattern Comparison Protocol (Working Standard, Draft), Structuria Evidentiary Tier System.

**Method note:** Each story was run independently through Checklist §2 (Structural Validity Gate, steps 1–6) before any tier or construction judgment was made, per the Checklist's own governing principle — *law alignment is confirmation, not classification.* Per Checklist §2's own rule, a case that fails any gate stops there; later gates are noted below only to show where the failure occurs, not to rescue the case. Where a story's authorship (the project author's own lived experience vs. an externally-sourced, independently-written account) is not stated, that ambiguity is flagged rather than assumed, per Checklist §4's own unresolved status.

---

### Story 1 (BIS monitor / mounting bracket)

- **Structural validity gate:**
  - *Real, datable event:* Pass. Reads as a specific, situated incident (a telemetry dropout, a named unit, a last-quarter-flagged port), not a hypothetical or composite.
  - *Sufficient concrete detail:* Pass. Cabling, network switch, the flaky port, the mounting bracket, the coffee machine on 3B are all specific enough to code without the reader supplying missing detail.
  - *Tested against all five domains:* See Domain fit below.
  - *Satisfies every required structural element of the best-fitting domain:* Fail. No domain's full element set is met (detail below).
  - *Adversarial reframe:* Fail. The entire account is fully explained by an ordinary, low-stakes social miscalibration — the narrator did a small favor, expected more warmth in return than a busy colleague had bandwidth to give, and felt deflated. No structural claim is required to explain any part of it.
  - *Would survive blind second-pass classification:* Not reached — gate already failed at steps 4–5.

- **Domain fit:** Tested against all five. **Peril** is the closest surface match (an assumption of camaraderie that "failed" on contact, a felt sense of disorientation afterward) but fails on substance: Peril requires real functioning insulation whose failure produces an outcome that *exceeds any single party's control* and reveals a structural truth. Here the "insulation" is a casual workplace assumption, the "outcome" is mild disappointment, fully within the narrator's own capacity to process and move past — not exposure in Peril's sense. **Boundaries** and **Cohesion** were considered and ruled out: there is no jurisdictional dispute (Boundaries) and no shared-system interdependence or colliding institutional models (Cohesion) — this is one person's private expectation not landing, not two parties' incompatible-but-reasonable models colliding. **Judgment** and **Stewardship** don't fit at all: no decision-under-uncertainty and no durational, custodial responsibility outlasting formal authority. No domain clears its required-elements bar.

- **Tentative Evidentiary Tier:** Per Checklist §2, this case fails the structural validity gate, so tier assignment is technically moot — a failed case isn't held at any tier, it's simply not usable as sourced evidence for a domain claim. If evaluated purely for evidentiary weight independent of the gate outcome: assuming first-person, self-sourced authorship, this would be **Tier 2, cued, not yet cross-checked** (Evidentiary Tier System §Tier 2) — nothing in the text indicates it predates a hypothesis or has been checked against a raw corpus. If the story is externally authored rather than the project author's own experience, it falls into the Case Validity Checklist §4 open question (hand-picked, externally-written stories don't cleanly fit Tier 1, 2, or 3) — unresolved here, per the Checklist's own framing, and would need that decision made before any tier could stick regardless of gate outcome.

- **Construction notes:** The two incidental details — the mounting bracket "with the tiny dent on the underside that nobody could explain," and the coffee machine remark the narrator is "still not sure why" was mentioned — read as inserted texture rather than organic recollection. Real memories don't usually flag their own inexplicability this neatly; it's the kind of detail a writer adds to simulate the texture of an unedited memory rather than a detail that actually arrives unedited. The closing paragraph ("Maybe I offered more than the situation could hold. Maybe it was nothing at all. I'm not sure yet, and I'm letting that uncertainty sit for now") is unusually composed for a raw field note — it resolves into a tidy, self-aware ambiguity rather than trailing off the way an actual unresolved feeling typically does on paper. Internally consistent; nothing contradicts itself. But the polish itself is the notable feature here, not the content.

- **Overall assessment:** Fails the structural validity gate on both the domain-fit and adversarial-reframe steps — a real-feeling but low-stakes social letdown, fully explicable without invoking any domain's physics. Not usable as sourced evidence in its current form.

### Story 2 (Renata / login credentials)

- **Structural validity gate:**
  - *Real, datable event:* Pass. Specific, recent, situated ("last month," a break room, a quarterly attribution report).
  - *Sufficient concrete detail:* Pass. The credentials issue, the ops coordinator, the timeline ("fixed within a day"), the later referral are concrete and codeable.
  - *Tested against all five domains:* See below.
  - *Satisfies every required structural element of the best-fitting domain:* Fail — see reasoning below.
  - *Adversarial reframe:* Fail. The whole account is explicable as an ordinary administrative correction (wrong login used, flagged, fixed in a day) plus the narrator's own retrospective emotional overlay about what closeness "means." The mundane account fully accounts for the events; the structural reading is supplied by the narrator's interpretation, not required by the facts.
  - *Would survive blind second-pass classification:* Not reached.

- **Domain fit:** Tested against all five. **Cohesion's Shared Frame** (invariant b) is the closest candidate — two individually reasonable models colliding (narrator: shared login is fine between close colleagues; Renata: individual accountability matters for the record) without either party being at fault. But Shared Frame requires two independently valid, comparably weighted institutional or systemic models in genuine tension — what's actually described here is a single, correctable process oversight on the narrator's side, resolved administratively in a day. That's a lighter-weight mismatch than the domain's other source cases (e.g., CASE-001, CASE-002) show; it doesn't clearly clear the bar of "mutually incompatible, both individually reasonable" at the same structural weight. **Peril** was considered — an assumed friendship "insulation" tested by a flat correction — but there's no loss of control and no consequence exceeding either party's ability to manage; it was fixed within a day and the relationship continued normally, undercutting Peril's required elements entirely. **Boundaries**, **Judgment**, and **Stewardship** don't fit: no jurisdictional dispute about what belongs to whom, no decision-under-uncertainty, no durational custodial trust question.

- **Tentative Evidentiary Tier:** Gate failure makes this moot, per the same reasoning as Story 1. If assessed independent of the gate: self-sourced first-person account with idiosyncratic detail (the specific smell that "clearly wasn't popcorn," the referral "unprompted") suggests **Tier 2, cued, not yet cross-checked** as the most likely fit if this is the author's own experience. If externally authored, the same Checklist §4 unresolved gap applies as in Story 1.

- **Construction notes:** Structurally almost identical in shape to Story 1: setup of assumed closeness → a flat, specific corrective moment → an odd sensory aside not doing narrative work (the burnt-popcorn smell) → a later ambiguous "positive" signal (the referral) offered as evidence but explicitly "not the one I'd been waiting for" → a closing two-hypothesis hedge ("I still can't tell if I misjudged... or if closeness just doesn't transfer"). That closing hedge is doing a lot of work and reads like a thesis statement rather than an organic trailing-off of thought. Internally consistent, no contradictions. The level of narrative tidiness — a clean single arc with a clean unresolved-but-articulate ending — is the most notable feature; it's cleaner than most real recollections tend to be when written down in the moment.

- **Overall assessment:** Fails the gate at the same two steps as Story 1 — the incident is fully explained as a minor, quickly-resolved administrative mismatch; the structural reading is imported by the narrator's own reflection rather than required by the facts.

### Story 3 (Vietnam / Halong Bay paddling)

- **Structural validity gate:**
  - *Real, datable event:* Pass. Specific trip, specific location (Halong Bay), specific activity (paddle boat vs. banana boat), stated as "earlier this year."
  - *Sufficient concrete detail:* Pass. The sister's seating choice, the "I made her do all the work" line, the parents' banana boat, the docking sequence are all specific and codeable.
  - *Tested against all five domains:* See below.
  - *Satisfies every required structural element of the best-fitting domain:* Fail.
  - *Adversarial reframe:* Fail. This is fully explicable as an ordinary sibling-relationship mismatch — different personalities, different expectations of a shared activity, a comment that landed harder than intended (or was intended to land hard). No structural mechanism is required.
  - *Would survive blind second-pass classification:* Not reached.

- **Domain fit:** Tested against all five. **Cohesion** (Structural Interdependence, invariant a) is the closest surface fit — a two-person task (paddling) requiring both parties to deliver their share, with a live dispute over whether that actually happened. But the domain's required elements aren't met: Structural Interdependence describes a system where the whole cannot proceed until each part delivers *its own piece independently* — this is a two-person recreational boat ride with an ambiguous, contested account of effort, not a case where a defined structural requirement went unmet in a way that caused visible functional failure. **Shared Frame** was also considered (brother's model: this is a bonding gesture; sister's model: he made me do the work) — plausible on the surface, but the story doesn't establish that both models were *independently reasonable given available information* the way Shared Frame's source cases do (CASE-001, CASE-002); it reads more as an emotionally loaded, single ambiguous comment than a genuine two-model structural mismatch. **Peril**, **Boundaries**, and **Stewardship** don't fit: no insulation-failure-and-exposure event, no jurisdictional dispute, no durational custodial trust question.

- **Tentative Evidentiary Tier:** Gate failure makes formal tier assignment moot. If assessed independent of the gate: this reads more plausibly as **Tier 2, uncued or lightly cued, not cross-checked** — nothing suggests it was constructed to match a pre-existing hypothesis, and its rougher phrasing (see below) is more consistent with an actual, first-draft recollection than a shaped illustrative case. If externally authored, the same §4 gap applies.

- **Construction notes:** Notably rougher than the other three, in a way that reads as more authentic rather than less. Grammatical looseness ("Its odd to say," "my parent's went on a different drier water activity") is the kind of unpolished construction a shaped illustrative narrative usually doesn't have. The detail that the sister avoided every pool the whole trip but chose to get wet here is a genuine, slightly odd inconsistency the narrator notices but doesn't resolve — that's a good sign of real observation rather than constructed narrative, since a constructed case would likely either omit it or resolve it. The closing line ("that experience reminded me while I don't [want to do more things with my sister]") is also less polished than the other three stories' endings — it reads like an actual sentence someone typed in one pass, not a workshopped closing thought.

- **Overall assessment:** Fails the gate on domain-fit and adversarial-reframe grounds, but of the four stories this one reads most like genuine, unshaped field material — the rougher construction is a point in its favor for authenticity even though it doesn't clear the structural bar for any domain.

### Story 4 (First date / wallet / repayment)

- **Structural validity gate:**
  - *Real, datable event:* Pass. Specific setting (a particular coffee shop), a specific sequence of events (misplaced wallet, retracing steps, dinner, a week-later repayment message).
  - *Sufficient concrete detail:* Pass. The exact repayment amount, the specific rejection message, the shift in tone during dinner are all concrete and codeable.
  - *Tested against all five domains:* See below.
  - *Satisfies every required structural element of the best-fitting domain:* Fail.
  - *Adversarial reframe:* Fail. Fully explicable as an ordinary case of one-sided romantic interest — he wasn't interested, he repaid the debt to close the loop cleanly, and the narrator felt more invested than the outcome reflected. Nothing here requires a structural claim.
  - *Would survive blind second-pass classification:* Not reached.

- **Domain fit:** Tested against all five. **Cohesion's Shared Frame** is again the closest surface candidate — narrator's model: a paid-for favor is a relationship gesture; his model: a debt to be settled exactly and then closed. But as in Story 3, the story doesn't establish that both models were independently reasonable in the domain's technical sense — it reads as one person's romantic hope colliding with the other person's simple disinterest, not two structurally valid, non-negotiable requirements from separate systems arriving in genuine conflict. **Peril** was considered given the language of exposure ("the meaning I attached... existed mostly in my own mind") but fails on stakes: a first date not working out doesn't involve real functioning insulation whose failure produces an outcome exceeding any party's control — it's an ordinary, low-consequence social outcome. **Boundaries**, **Judgment**, and **Stewardship** don't fit for the same reasons as the other three stories: no jurisdictional dispute, no decision-under-uncertainty, no durational custodial trust question.

- **Tentative Evidentiary Tier:** Gate failure makes formal assignment moot. If assessed independent of the gate: most plausibly **Tier 2, cued, not yet cross-checked** if self-sourced, given the same tight, single-arc construction seen in Stories 1 and 2. If externally authored, the same §4 gap applies and is, if anything, more live here given how literary the prose is (see below).

- **Construction notes:** The most polished of the four by a clear margin. The chalkboard menu, the jar of mismatched stir sticks, the bench with peeling green paint, and above all the repayment detail ("Not rounded up, not rounded down. Exact.") are the kind of precise, pointed details a short-story writer chooses deliberately for effect — they're vivid, but they don't carry the incidental, slightly-irrelevant texture of an unplanned memory the way Story 3's pool-avoidance detail does. The closing line ("I am just less certain than I once was about what kindness means to the person receiving it") is a clean thematic statement, essentially interchangeable in function with Story 1 and Story 2's closing hedges. Internally consistent and well-constructed, but "well-constructed" is itself the flag here — this reads more like a finished piece of short fiction built around a thesis than a raw account written to work through an actual, unresolved memory.

- **Overall assessment:** Fails the gate on the same two steps as the others — a mundane, fully-explicable account of one-sided romantic interest, notable mainly for how literary and thesis-driven its construction is.

---

## Summary Note

All four stories passed the first two gate steps (real/datable, sufficiently specific) but failed at steps 4–5 (full required-element satisfaction for any domain, and the adversarial reframe) independently, on their own individual merits and reasoning. None is currently usable as sourced case material for any of the five domains without either (a) more information establishing genuinely structural — not merely emotionally significant — stakes, or (b) a stronger case that an ordinary, non-structural account doesn't fully explain what happened. Per Checklist §4, evidentiary tier remains formally unresolved for any of these regardless, pending clarity on authorship (project author's own experience vs. externally-sourced narrative) and, separately, pending Case Validity Checklist §4's own open design question about where hand-picked, independently-written stories belong in the Evidentiary Tier System at all.
