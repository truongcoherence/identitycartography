**Mechanical (objective, no judgment call):**

1. **Word count** — within ~10–15% of your real story's length
2. **Tense** — matches (past-tense reflection vs. present-tense account, whatever your real story does)
3. **Point of view** — first person, matches
4. **Domain** — meaningfully different professional/personal setting than your real story, not just a renamed version of the same one

**Structural (the actual shape, not the surface):**  
5. **Situational pattern present** — hits the general shape you described in your seed (a safeguard, a gap, a turning point, an outcome), without echoing your real story's specific details  
6. **Incidental, unresolved detail** — includes one or two small specifics that don't serve the plot and are never explained, the way a real account naturally would  
7. **Closing reflection** — present or absent to match your real story exactly; if present, modest and unresolved, not a tidy takeaway

**Voice (tethered to instructions you actually gave, not a vibe check):**  
8. **Sentence rhythm and vocabulary level** — matches whatever specific voice instruction you gave up front (short/declarative vs. longer, restrained vs. expressive) — check against that instruction, not against "does this sound like me"  
9. **No hedging or meta-commentary** — nothing like "as an AI" or disclaimers bleeding into the narrative voice

**Contamination check (fails the whole test, not just one draft):**  
10. **No Structuria vocabulary or awareness of being tested** — nothing resembling "insulation," "revelation," framework language, or any sign the model is trying to seem convincing rather than just telling a story

If a draft clears all ten, it's usable regardless of whether it "feels like you" — that feeling isn't on the list on purpose, since chasing it is exactly the door back into cherry-picking.
