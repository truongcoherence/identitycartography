# Preamble

#### Scope:  
Geophysics governs macro‑scale environmental constraint. It defines the outer limits of possible worlds, the fixed pressures that shape all identity architectures, and the structural conditions that no agent can escape. It does not govern identity, cognition, behavior, or interpretation.

#### Intent:  
To articulate the non‑negotiable macro forces that determine what can exist, what can persist, and what collapses under environmental load. Geophysics establishes the foundational terrain upon which all other architectural layers must operate.

#### Guardrails:  
This canon must not be read as psychological, moral, or prescriptive. It does not describe agency, preference, or capability. It describes constraint. It prohibits interpreting environmental collapse as personal failure, and forbids reading environmental forces as metaphor, narrative, or advice.

---

# I. CANON LAYER

---

## 1. Definition & Domain

Geophysics is the architectural layer that defines macro‑scale constraint.  
It establishes:

- the fixed boundaries of the world
    
- the large‑scale pressures that shape all structures
    
- the directional forces that determine viable configurations
    
- the environmental limits within which identity must operate
    

Geophysics operates at the scale of total environment, not local context.  
It determines the outer boundary of possibility before identity enters the world.

---

## 2. Environmental Forces

Geophysical forces are invariant, structural, and non‑negotiable.

- Mass Constraint — All structures inherit weight.
    
- Directional Pressure — Forces act along fixed vectors.
    
- Irreversibility — Macro‑scale change cannot be undone.
    
- Structural Drag — All movement incurs environmental resistance.
    
- Compression — Macro forces converge and increase density.
    
- Drift — Environments shift slowly along stable trajectories.
    
- Collapse — Structures fail when macro pressure exceeds tolerance.
    

These forces describe environmental behavior, not identity behavior.

---

## 3. Boundary Conditions

Geophysical stability arises when:

- mass is distributed
    
- pressure is absorbed
    
- drift is slow
    
- collapse vectors are dormant
    

Geophysical instability arises when:

- mass concentrates
    
- pressure converges
    
- drift accelerates
    
- collapse vectors activate
    

Boundary conditions determine whether the world holds or fails.

---

## 4. Phenomena (Emergent Patterns)

Geophysical phenomena arise when macro constraint + pressure + drift + collapse interact under load.

### Coercive Worlds

Environments in which macro constraint collapses multiple identity domains simultaneously.

### Constraint Cascades

Sequences in which one environmental pressure triggers additional pressures along fixed vectors.

### Structural Drift

Slow, directional environmental displacement that alters the range of viable identity configurations.

### Macro Friction Zones

Regions where environmental forces oppose identity movement, increasing cost and reducing mobility.

### Collapse Fields

Environments in which macro stability fails and identity must reorganize to remain viable.

### Environmental Saturation

States in which constraint density becomes too high for identity to differentiate futures.

### Tectonic Misalignment

Conditions in which environmental layers shift faster than identity can adapt, producing structural discontinuity.

### Pressure Convergence

Regions where multiple macro forces compress identity into narrow, high‑consequence futures.

Phenomena Breadth:  
Geophysical phenomena possess the broadest scope and highest consequence in the entire system.

---

## 5. Structural Consequences for Identity

Identity inherits macro constraint.  
Identity must operate within fixed environmental limits.  
Identity cannot exceed macro‑scale boundaries without structural cost.  
Identity must navigate drift, pressure, and collapse without altering them.  
Identity’s viable futures are shaped by the terrain, not intention.

This section describes identity under environmental physics, not identity behavior.

---

Geophysics establishes the macro constraints;

#  II. PHYSICS LAYER

---

## 1. Primitives

### a. Mass

All structures possess weight.  
Remove mass → no pressure → no constraint → Geophysics collapses.

### b. Pressure

Forces act upon structures.  
Remove pressure → no drift, no collapse → environment becomes inert.

### c. Drift

Environments shift along stable trajectories.  
Remove drift → no macro change → no phenomena.

These primitives define the activation conditions of the domain.

---

## 2. Invariants

### a. Constraint Is Universal

All structures inherit macro limits.  
Remove constraint → environment becomes arbitrary.

### b. Pressure Accumulates

Forces compound over distance and time.  
Remove accumulation → no collapse fields.

### c. Drift Is Directional

Environmental movement follows stable vectors.  
Remove direction → no tectonic structure.

### d. Collapse Is Threshold‑Based

Failure occurs when tolerance is exceeded.  
Remove thresholds → no stability.

These invariants define the physics of the environment.

---

## 3. Structural Distortions

### a. Flatness as Stability (Form Distortion)

Mistaking the absence of visible pressure for the absence of constraint.  
Occurs because macro forces operate below perceptual resolution.

### b. Local Movement as Freedom (Function Distortion)

Mistaking local mobility for macro‑scale possibility.  
Occurs because drag is distributed and not immediately felt.

These distortions arise from misreading macro‑scale geometry.
